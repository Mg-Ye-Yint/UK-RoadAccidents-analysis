🔍 Key Insights

Slight casualties dominate (84.1%), indicating most accidents are less severe but highly frequent.

Cars account for ~80% of casualties, making them the primary contributor to road incidents.

Single carriageways have the highest accident rates, far exceeding other road types.

Urban areas record more casualties than rural areas, likely due to higher traffic density.

Most accidents occur in daylight, suggesting volume of traffic is a bigger factor than visibility.

Dry road conditions still show the highest accidents, meaning weather is not the main driver.

Consistent monthly trends across 2021–2022, with slight peaks toward late months (Oct–Nov).

💡 Recommendations

Improve traffic management on single carriageways (speed control, lane discipline, signage).

Focus on car driver awareness campaigns, as they represent the majority of incidents.

Enhance urban road safety measures (better crossings, signals, congestion control).

Implement preventive measures during peak months (extra monitoring, campaigns).

Strengthen general driving behavior initiatives, since most accidents happen in normal (dry/daylight) conditions.

Promote defensive driving and stricter rule enforcement to reduce frequent minor accidents.
